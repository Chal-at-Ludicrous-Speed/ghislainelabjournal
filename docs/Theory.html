1 Theory

If there is an effect of departmental prestige on the formation of interuniversity collaboration networks present, then it would also be reflected on the individual level. Sociologists who have a lower h-index could then benefit from the collective prestige of their department. This is called basking in reflective glory, which is a well known effect within the social networks literature.

The principle of preferential attachment (Hâncean 2014), who quotes (Moody 2004) and someone else). “New links and nodes are not randomly connected to large networks, but based on observed power-law distributions” (page number). Matthew-effect: scientific fields grow and develop around highly prestigious scientific scholars. These highly prestigious scholars are star nodes (source).

A prestigious ego receives more ties than they send themselves (Knoke 2011).

H1. Sociologists prefer working with a sociologist with a higher individual prestige than themselves H2. Sociologists prefer working with a sociologist with a higher departmental prestige than themselves

With Hypothesis 2, it can either indicate that a sociologist has an equal or higher h-index than the department mean, or the sociologist in question has a lower h-index than the mean and can benefit from the department’s prestige.

Hâncean, Perc, M. 2014. “Fragmented Romanian Sociology: Growth and Structure of the Collaboration Network.” PLOS One 9: 1–9.
Knoke, S., D. & Yang. 2011. SAGE Publications.
Moody, J. 2004. “The Structure of a Social Science Collaboration n Network: Disciplinary Cohesion from 1963 to 1999.” American Sociological Review 69: 213–38.
